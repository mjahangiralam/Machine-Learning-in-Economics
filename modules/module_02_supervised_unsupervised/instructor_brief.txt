MODULE 2: Supervised and Unsupervised Learning in Economics

A. Module Description
Students learn the economist’s lens on supervised learning (prediction, loss functions, train/validation splits, overfitting) and unsupervised learning (clustering, dimensionality reduction) as tools for describing structure in data. The module connects high-dimensional prediction in asset pricing (Gu, Kelly, and Xiu, 2020) to broader empirical workflows while emphasizing that unsupervised outputs require careful economic labeling.

B. Learning Objectives
1. Implement supervised regression with out-of-sample evaluation using cross-validation.
2. Interpret bias–variance tradeoffs intuitively and connect them to overfitting in economic panels.
3. Apply PCA and k-means to simulated “many characteristics” data and discuss interpretability limits.
4. Relate the workflow to Mullainathan and Spiess (2017) and Gu, Kelly, and Xiu (2020).

C. Student/Group Assignment
Present both reference papers at a high level; teach train/validation/test logic; demonstrate Colab on simulated asset-characteristic-like data; propose how supervised prediction or PCA/k-means features could connect to Autor, Dorn, and Hanson (2013) (e.g., vulnerability prediction or market segmentation) without overclaiming causality.
