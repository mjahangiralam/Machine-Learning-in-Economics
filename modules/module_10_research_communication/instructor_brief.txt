MODULE 10: Research Communication

A. Module Description
Students learn to communicate empirical work to academic and policy audiences through slides, a structured five-page two-column report, and a conference-style poster. Emphasis is placed on clarity of estimands, honest limitations, reproducibility links, and visual hierarchy.

B. Learning Objectives
1. Structure a 10–12 minute talk: motivation, design, main result, ML extension, limitations.
2. Apply poster design principles: one main message, readable figures, QR code to code/data.
3. Write an abstract-style summary avoiding causal overclaiming.
4. Practice Q\&A strategies for identification challenges and ML critique.

C. Student/Group Assignment
Draft poster layout (digital), rehearse timed talk, exchange peer feedback using a rubric (clarity, accuracy, design).
